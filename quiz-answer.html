What are the advantages of abundant capillaries, mitochondria, & myglobin in Type I muscle?
In Type I muscle, the abundant mitochondria generate ATP, which fuels the cellular process, while the capillaries and myoglobin fuel the cells with oxygen.

ANSWER EXPLANATION
Muscle is a soft tissue derived from the mesoderm. The primary types of muscle are skeletal, cardiac, and smooth muscle. Skeletal muscle cells are either fast twitch (white), slow twitch (red), or a rare pink intermediate.
Type I, slow twitch, muscle cells appear red in color due to abundant capillaries, mitochondria, and myoglobin. These features allow Type I muscle to undergo aerobic respiration and contract for long periods with little force.
In Type I muscle, the abundant mitochondria generate ATP, which fuels the cellular process, while the capillaries and myoglobin fuel the cells with oxygen.
